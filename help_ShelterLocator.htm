How to use the Shelter Locator

The Shelter Locator application helps citizens locate and obtain information about services available at shelters in their community. This application could be used to share information about shelters in a community that are always available to citizens that require support or stood up in time of emergency to communicate about new shelter locations available to house displaced citizens.

To locate a shelter, simply enter an address or shelter name in the search box, or select amentities. Optionally, you can use your current location to find a shelter near you. The shelter(s) will then be highlighted on the map and relevant information about available support services presented to you.

Using the Application

The Shelter Locator application provides residents an easy way to find shelters near them or a shelter by its name. Enter your address or click on the Current Location button to find shelters near you. If you click the Current Location button, it will use the location of your computer's IP address or your cell phone/tablet location to locate shelters near you. When you enter an address or use your current location, the application will present a list of shelters near you and allow you to review relevant information about each shelter. In addition, you can search for a shelter, or specific amenity you require, by name. To do that, enter the name of the shelter in the search box. When you enter the name of the shelter, the application will find the shelter or present a list of shelters that offer the aminity you require and allow you to review relevant information about each shelter.

Once you have found a shelter you are interested in, click the "Add to My List" button in the info-popup window or in the information panel. Once shelters have been added to your list, you can view that list by clicking the "My List" button. From there, you can calculate a route between them or print your list of shelters for later use.

Finding an Area of Interest

There are three primary ways to identify an area of interest. You can interact directly with the map and click on a shelter when you've located it. You can also use your current location or an address to find a shelter near you. Finally, you can search for a shelter by name.

Map Based Navigation

There are several ways to interact with the map:

1. Use the basemap switcher to select the basemap you'd like to interact with
2. Hold down the shift key and drag a box on the map to zoom in
3. Hold Down Ctrl+Shift to zoom out
4. Roll your mouse roller to zoom in/out
5. Click the + or - to zoom in/out

Searching

The search box can be used to find an address or shelter you're interested in:

1. Enter the address or shelter name you're interested in
2. Click on the item in the list to locate the address or click on the shelter in the list on the left panel

You can enter a partial address and then identify the correct value in the list.

The Amenities panel can be used to search for shelters by amenity or service provided:

1. Click on each amentity you are intersted in and click search.

Directions

You can also get directions to each shelter using your current location or an address you select. When you find a shelter you'd like to visit, enter an address to start from, or click the use current location button, to calculate the directions and route.

Share Your Map

You can click on the Share Map button to share your map several different ways. You can compose an email, post to your Facebook wall or post a Tweet. When you use the Facebook or Twitter links, you'll be asked to sign in to your respective account.

More Information

The Shelter Locator application can used by Emergency Response or Government organizations responsible for managing shelters to deploy a web-based shelter locator application. This application provides 24x7 access to the information and typically supplements citizen engagement phone numbers staffed by these organizations.
